Melbourne Propery Market Overview

This visualisation is a quick overview of the Melbourne's property market from 2016 to 2018

The Australian Housing market has been performing strongly in the past decade. With 6.8% annual growth for houses and 5.9% annual growth for apartments. Let us take a deeper dive into the property market.

It came as no suprise that there are more houses on sale than apartments. Apartment sales make up only around 20% of all dwellings in Australia.

Between 2016 - 2018, there were 17982 properties sold publicly and only 3054 of those were apartments, a merely 17% of total sales.

Let us look at the performances of each individual suburbs across different types and years. Choropleth map below displays Median prices of different suburbs over the years. The bar chart shows all the properties sold with the price ranges.

As expected, the properties market has been rising steadily over the time period. Espically the South-East suburbs next to the city. Most popular price ranges for houses have also stepped up from 600k-800k to 800k-1M dollors.

Lastly, the heat map on the right visuailzes the relationship between number of rooms and distance to CBD out of all sales record.

Most popular houses are around 3 bedrooms, between 6-17km from the CBD. On the other hand, apartments sold are usually 1-2 bedrooms and within 2-14km from the CBD.
